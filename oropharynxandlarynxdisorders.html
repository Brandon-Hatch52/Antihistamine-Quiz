Disorders of the Oropharynx and Larynx – Quiz

Graduate-Level PA Assessment

Question 1

Objective(s) assessed: Differential diagnosis of acute sore throat

ENT-O&L-1

Stem:
A 22-year-old college student presents with sore throat, rhinorrhea, cough, and conjunctivitis. Physical exam shows mild pharyngeal erythema with scant exudate. Which diagnosis is most likely?

  • A. Streptococcal pharyngitis
  • B. EBV pharyngitis
  • C. Viral pharyngitis
  • D. Diphtheria

Correct answer: C

Viral pharyngitis commonly presents with URI symptoms such as cough, rhinorrhea, and conjunctivitis. Streptococcal pharyngitis typically lacks cough and rhinorrhea.

Question 2

Objective(s) assessed: Viral pharyngitis management

ENT-O&L-2

Stem:
A patient with uncomplicated viral pharyngitis asks for antibiotics “to speed recovery.” What is the most appropriate response?

  • A. Prescribe amoxicillin for 10 days
  • B. Prescribe azithromycin
  • C. Recommend supportive care only
  • D. Prescribe clindamycin

Correct answer: C

Viral pharyngitis is self-limited and treated with hydration, NSAIDs, and reassurance. Antibiotics provide no benefit and increase adverse effects.

Question 3

Objective(s) assessed: EBV epidemiology and presentation

ENT-O&L-2

Stem:
A 19-year-old presents with fatigue, sore throat, posterior cervical lymphadenopathy, and splenomegaly. Which laboratory finding most strongly supports the diagnosis?

  • A. Neutrophilia
  • B. Lymphocytosis
  • C. Elevated CRP only
  • D. Positive rapid strep test

Correct answer: B

EBV infectious mononucleosis commonly causes lymphocytosis. Neutrophilia is more consistent with bacterial infection.

Question 4

Objective(s) assessed: EBV diagnostic testing

Which test is most commonly used to confirm EBV infection in a symptomatic patient?

  • A. Rapid strep antigen test
  • B. Throat culture
  • C. Heterophile antibody test
  • D. Procalcitonin level

Correct answer: C

The heterophile antibody (Monospot) test is commonly used and has high specificity in symptomatic patients.

Question 5

Objective(s) assessed: Streptococcal pharyngitis diagnosis

Question 16
